# Rescue Home Offers Content Calendar v2
## Situation-Based Educational Content

---

## The Core Idea

Every post answers: **"I'm in [SITUATION]. What are ALL my options?"**

The education is about their specific problem, and naturally shows why traditional options sometimes don't work - which is when our creative solutions make sense.

---

## 12-Week Content Calendar

### CLUSTER 3: Distressed Situations (Weeks 1-4)

**Week 1: "My House Needs $50K+ in Repairs - Can I Still Sell It?"**
- Educate on: Selling as-is, repair costs vs. value added, FHA 203k buyers, investor buyers, fix-and-flip partnerships
- Their situation: House has major issues (roof, foundation, HVAC, mold)
- Connects to: Cash offer, fix-and-flip partnership, as-is listing

**Week 2: "I'm 3 Months Behind on My Mortgage - What Happens Next?"**
- Educate on: Foreclosure timeline in Nevada, reinstatement, forbearance, loan modification, short sale, subject-to
- Their situation: Behind on payments, scared, don't know timeline
- Connects to: Subject-to, short sale, cash offer to beat auction

**Week 3: "My House Has Been on the Market for 90 Days - Now What?"**
- Educate on: Why listings go stale, the stigma effect, price reduction strategy, relisting, alternative options
- Their situation: Listed with agent, not selling, frustrated
- Connects to: Cash offer, creative solutions, price reality check

**Week 4: "I Need to Sell in 30 Days or Less - Is That Even Possible?"**
- Educate on: Realistic timelines for each selling method, what speeds things up, what slows things down
- Their situation: Job relocation, divorce deadline, foreclosure auction date, estate deadline
- Connects to: Cash offer (7-14 days), subject-to, all fast-close options

---

### CLUSTER 4: Life Situation Changes (Weeks 5-8)

**Week 5: "Selling the House in a Divorce - Who Decides What?"**
- Educate on: Nevada community property, court orders, buyout math, forced sale, what happens if you can't agree
- Their situation: Divorcing, house is the biggest asset, emotions high
- Connects to: Fast cash sale, buyout financing options, neutral third party

**Week 6: "I Inherited a House I Don't Want - What Are My Options?"**
- Educate on: Probate process, stepped-up basis, holding costs, out-of-state ownership challenges
- Their situation: Inherited property, don't want to be landlord, may need repairs
- Connects to: Cash offer, as-is sale, property management referral

**Week 7: "I'm a Tired Landlord - How Do I Get Out?"**
- Educate on: Selling with tenants, lease obligations, investor buyers vs. owner-occupant buyers, 1031 exchange
- Their situation: Done with landlording, bad tenants, property needs work
- Connects to: Cash offer, sell to investor, subject-to if they have good rate

**Week 8: "Relocating for Work - Sell or Rent My Las Vegas House?"**
- Educate on: Long-distance landlording realities, property management costs, selling costs, market timing
- Their situation: Moving, need to decide fast, don't want two mortgages
- Connects to: Fast sale, lease-option, property management referral

---

### CLUSTER 5: Financial Situations (Weeks 9-12)

**Week 9: "I Owe More Than My House is Worth - Am I Stuck?"**
- Educate on: Underwater options, short sale process, waiting it out, bringing cash to close, deed in lieu
- Their situation: Negative equity, can't sell traditionally, feel trapped
- Connects to: Short sale assistance, subject-to, creative solutions

**Week 10: "Can I Sell My House If I Have a Lien or Judgment?"**
- Educate on: Types of liens, how to find them, negotiating with lien holders, selling with liens
- Their situation: IRS lien, contractor lien, judgment, back taxes
- Connects to: Cash offer (we handle liens), title clearing assistance

**Week 11: "What If I Can't Afford to Make Repairs Before Selling?"**
- Educate on: Repair vs. price reduction math, as-is disclosure, buyer credits, investor buyers
- Their situation: Know house needs work, don't have cash to fix it
- Connects to: As-is cash offer, fix-and-flip partnership, repair credit negotiation

**Week 12: "Selling a House with Code Violations or Unpermitted Work"**
- Educate on: Disclosure requirements, permit costs, buyer concerns, investor buyers who don't care
- Their situation: Unpermitted addition, code violations, worried about liability
- Connects to: Cash offer (we buy as-is), permit resolution options

## Post Structure Template

**Opening (100 words):**
"If you're reading this, you're probably [in this exact situation]. You're not alone - we talk to homeowners in your position every week. Here's what you need to know about ALL your options."

**Section 1: Understanding Your Situation**
- What's really happening
- Timeline/urgency factors
- Common misconceptions

**Section 2: The Traditional Option**
- How it works
- When it makes sense
- Why it might not work for you (honest assessment)

**Section 3: Your Other Options**
- Alternative solutions explained
- Pros and cons of each
- Real numbers/examples

**Section 4: How to Decide**
- Questions to ask yourself
- What matters most (speed, price, certainty, simplicity)

**Closing CTA:**
"Every situation is different. If you want to see what all your options look like with real numbers, get your Second Opinion. We'll show you everything - even if the best option isn't us."
